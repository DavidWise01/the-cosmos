What "0,0" means — it's two different centers

In the gyroscope, the center is the fixed pivot — the point everything rotates around, the one place that stays still. That's where the dot sits.

In the Bloch picture, the center (r = 0) is the maximally mixed state — a qubit with no definite orientation at all. Same "middle," opposite meaning. Don't conflate them: one is a still point, the other is a state of total uncertainty.

The real parallel — why this isn't a costume

A spinning gyro has angular momentum along its axis; a torque makes the axis precess (sweep a cone) instead of toppling — dL/dt = torque.

A qubit in a field obeys dr/dt = r × (field): the Bloch vector sweeps a cone at the Larmor frequency, and a driving field gives Rabi oscillations. Same equation — a vector crossed with an axis. That's the genuine link behind running them side by side.

Honest footing. This is an illustration of precession, not a measurement of anything — the dot is a marker at the origin, and the gyro↔qubit correspondence is the real, well-known physics (Larmor / Rabi precession), drawn here to scale the analogy honestly. The ephemeral dot, as ever, only does what it's placed to do: hold the still center while the rest turns.
