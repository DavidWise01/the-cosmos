Fifteen pamphlets, built one conversation at a time. Each carries its own honest colophon — flagging where myth is myth, where a "code" is a modern invention, where a tidy phrase came later than it pretends, where a borrowed frame doesn't quite fit, where a sudden "emergence" is really just the ruler, and where a beautiful physics is still only a hypothesis. The through-line is impermanence — and, at the end, three machines-and-physics pamphlets asking the same question the mythos did: is the whole ever truly more than its parts, or only the parts, dancing in step?
